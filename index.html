A tree-map mocked up in Tableau, then recreated in Photoshop Elements to create the medicine tablet shapes.

2018

This visualisation was created from a #MakeoverMonday dataset. The original visualisation can be found here.

At first I envisaged a network diagram showing flows of exports from one country to another. My second idea was a slope chart comparing exporters with consumers. Both plans changed when I realised the dataset didn't contain the level of detail required.

Returning to the original visualisation, I realised that the aim was to give a sense of scale to the exports by country. As a parts to whole relationship, this inspired me to try a tree-map. After a little data prep (the data included categories for ship stores and bunkers!) I mocked it up in Tableau. Immediately I was struck with the idea that the squares looked like tablets.

By now I'd missed the Makeover Monday deadline - but I couldn't get the idea out of my head. I took a screenshot of the Tableau treemap and opened in Photoshop Elements, where I used it as a template to draw tablet shapes, using drop shadows and bevel styles to create the 3D effect.

I changed the colour scheme to colours that reminded me of medicine, ensuring that all countries within a region were similar in colour. The predominance of European countries really stood out, providing the key insight which I emphasised with a colour-matched sub-heading.
